Dangote Group

Assessing options for operating sustainably amid political and economic volatility.

Context

This global operations strategy case study examines Dangote Group's approach to scaling and sustaining industrial operations across emerging markets, with a focus on cement manufacturing in Nigeria. As Africa's largest industrial conglomerate, Dangote operates in environments shaped by infrastructure constraints, supply chain volatility, regulatory complexity, and currency risk. The work explored how Dangote's vertically integrated operating model supports resilience, cost control, and market leadership while navigating the operational realities of large-scale production in developing economies.

Goals

The primary goal was to evaluate how Dangote's vertically integrated operating model supports large-scale production and cost leadership in challenging operating environments. Secondary goals included assessing risks related to infrastructure reliability, logistics, and currency exposure, and identifying how operational strategy enables resilience and long-term competitiveness across markets.

How I Worked

The work was conducted as a team-based global operations analysis, examining how Dangote manages production, logistics, and supply chain coordination across a complex operating environment. I analyzed the company's vertically integrated model, including sourcing, manufacturing, and distribution, and evaluated how external factors such as infrastructure constraints and currency volatility influence operating decisions. I worked with the team to synthesize these insights into a structured assessment of operational resilience and contributed to developing the final presentation.

Key Decisions & Tradeoffs

A central decision examined was Dangote's reliance on vertical integration to maintain cost control and supply reliability in infrastructure-constrained environments, accepting higher capital intensity in return. Another key tradeoff involved the decision to invest in large-scale assets such as the refinery, prioritizing long-term self-sufficiency and domestic value capture over faster, lower-risk expansion. These choices shaped recommendations focused on execution discipline and risk management within the existing operating model, including selective use of AI.

Impact

The analysis clarified how Dangote's vertically integrated operations enable cost leadership and resilience in challenging operating environments. The work highlighted key operational risks and strategic tradeoffs tied to capital-intensive investments, including the refinery, and articulated how disciplined execution could sustain long-term competitiveness. The final output delivered an executive-ready perspective on how operations strategy supports scale and market leadership across emerging markets.

What This

It sharpened my judgment around balancing long-term capital commitments with execution risk, and reinforced how strategic discipline and operational rigor support scale and resilience.
